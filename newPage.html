5 Benefits of playing Chess

Home Resume
  1. Can help improve cognitive skills such as:
    • Memory: In order to be a successful chess player you must learn moves and combinations established by chess masters.
    • Planning: You have to start with a plan and be able to adapt as the game goes.
    • Problem solving: When your plan doesn't go they way you would've liked, you must come up with a solution as you play.
    • Patience: Chess players need to sit down for hours in silence which requires careful contemplation and analysis.


  2. Helps you build healthy social relationships:
    • It allows you to see things from a different perspective.
    • Chess players need to be able to anticipate their opponents moves.
    • Chess players learn their opponent's playing style and adapt to it in order to win.
    • It allows players to create empathy and respect for other players.


  3. Chess elevates your creativity:
    • Chess players learn to interpret patterns and abstract forms.
    • They are also able to exercise creative thinking while trying to find solutions as problems come.


  4. Chess allows you to increase self-awareness:
    • During a chess game you can't allow your emotions to take control over you. Therefore, you learn to auto-regulate yourself.
    • It helps you develop resilience because it pushes you to find solutions until the end.
    • You are able to recognize your own weaknesses and improve.


  5. Chess may protect you against dementia and other mental health diseases:
    • Chess demands complex mental flexibilty which increases mental capacity and could protect the brain from cognitive decline.
    • It promotes focus and attention which has proven to decrease some ADHD symptoms.
    • It increases the sense of calm which can lower the risk of having a panic attack.
