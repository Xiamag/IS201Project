A Little Bit of History

Guanajuato is the capital of the state of Guanajuato, one the 32 states that make up the country of Mexico. The name Guanajuato derives from the Purepecha word "Cuanaxhuato" which means "mountainous place of frogs" and it lives up to its name with beautiful hills.

Its silver mines have been the base of this city's economy for hundred of years. Gold, tin, copper, lead, mercury and opals are also found inside the mines and you can it for yourself by going to one of the tours that take place there everyday.

Although, another big part of it's economy are the tourists that come every years from other parts of Mexico and the world. The houses of Guanajuato provide exquisite colour to the city and hipnotized everyone who walks its streets. There’s no subtle, complementary colour scheme—fuchsia pink mingles with pillar box red, saffron yellow, baby blue, and lime green.

The vibe is young and urban with a mix of traditional music, buildings, and costumes that allow you to travel back to the 1800's. Let your spirit free and get lost in the beauty of its streets. You might come across incredible urban artists and delightful food and music at every corner.
